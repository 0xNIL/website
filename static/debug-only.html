Introducing the NIL
An experimental eventually-valuable token, initially priced at zero
Why?

Tokens sold with an ICO, usually, are backed by a service which pretends to guarantee that the token will eventually increase its value.

Against this claim, many recent ICOs have demonstrated that the declared value of a token is irrelevant for the market.
Having seen this, the thesis that 0xNIL wants to verify is that

Token's prices are arbitrary and depend on a perception of their value which has little to do with their declared value.

How?

Introducing the NIL, a token with a price set at zero during its Token Distribution Event (TDE).

During the TDE, those who send 0 ether to the contract address will have back 1000 NIL. To have more NIL, just send 0 ether again, and so on.

If at the closing of the TDE, the NIL can be traded, the market will set a price. In this case, any future earning will be a 100% gain.

When?

The code for the TDE can be audited on Github.

As soon as everything is ready, a date for the TDE will be defined in the last quarter of 2017.

It will last about one week.

More info about the 0xNIL project in this blog post.

Three more points about 0xNIL

The ICOs madness has brought to the market successful tokens, like the GNT, as well as struggling tokens, like BAT and BNT. If the NIL will find a place in the market, it will set a minimum level that others have to satisfy.

If the NIL will be traded, any company which wants to sell a token in a crowd sale has to give better justification for why they are raising money. If not, it will be compared with this special token given for free.

The success of useless tokens, like the EUT, has already shown that investors don't pay attention to the presentation. The NIL is a further step to push the experiment to the extreme, because here nothing is sold.

And the most important:
There was a time where the best services on the centralized Internet required a payment. At some point, something happened and companies started offering great services for free. It's not hard to imagine a future where companies and institutions will distribute coins for free. The NIL could be a precedent for those future coins, which, used in the right way, have the potential to reduce the inequality and create a more just society.
NIL Token Distribution Event

The TDE will be in Q4/2017 and will last 30,000 blocks in the Ethereum Blockchain, which means about one week.

The price for 1000 NIL is fixed in 0 ether or, for wallets which don't allow to send 0 ether, 1 wei. Transactions with more than 1 wei will be rejected.

To have more NIL, just send 0 ether again, and so on, until reaching the maximum of 10000 NIL per wallet.

There will be a 40% bonus for participants in the first cluster of 10,000 blocks, and a 20% bonus for participants in the second cluster.

A 20% tip will be reserved to the development team. As long as the NIL price is zero, the tip is literally nil.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 0xNIL a social art project?

When initially I had the idea, it was a reaction to the craziness of the Bancor ICO. How is it possible that someone can raise so much money without a solid product behind it? How can people purchase $153M of BATs without any guarantee that that investment makes any sense? After thinking of many artistic ways to express my resentment, I figured out that the best action was to use a similar approach and "sell" a free token in a similar process. So, the intention was artistic. All the technicality came after.

How is 0xNIL different than Ethereum Useless Token?

Don't be fooled by the smart selling strategy adopted by the UET creator. Even if s/he talks about no value, the value of the UET has been set when a price has been fixed for it. This means that the UET was never useless and, despite what they say, has a well defined initial value.
The NIL, instead, has a price fixed at zero and an initial value of zero because of that, which also means that there is no guarantee at all that it will eventually have a value, and this is what the 0xNIL experiment is about.
