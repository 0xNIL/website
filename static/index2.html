Introducing the NIL
An experimental eventually-valuable token, initially priced at zero
Why?

Tokens sold with an ICO, usually, are backed by a service which pretends to guarantee that the token will eventually increase its value.

Against this claim, many recent ICOs have demonstrated that the declared value of a token is irrelevant for the market.
Having seen this, the thesis that 0xNIL wants to verify is that

Token's prices are arbitrary and depend on a perception of their value which has little to do with their declared value.

How?

Introducing the NIL, a token with a price set at zero during its Initial Free Distribution (IFD), a process technically similar to an ICO.

During the IFD, those who send 0 ether to the contract address will have back 100 NIL. To have more NIL, just send 0 ether again, and so on, until reaching the maximum of 1000 NIL per wallet.

At the closing of the IFD, the NIL will be put on the exchanges to set a price. Any future earning will be a 100% gain.

When?

The code for the IFD can be audited on Github.

As soon as everything is ready, a date for the IFD will be defined in the last quarter of 2017.

It will last about one week.

More info about the 0xNIL project in this blog post.

Three more points about 0xNIL

The ICOs madness has brought to the market successful tokens, like GNT and DNT, as well as struggling tokens, like BAT and BNT. If the NIL will find a place in the market, it will set a minimum level that others have to satisfy.

If the NIL will be traded as expected, any company which wants to sell a token in a crowd sale has to give better justification for why they are raising money. If not, it will be compared with this special token given for free.

The success of useless tokens, like the EUT, has already shown that investors don't pay attention to the presentation. The NIL is a further step to push the experiment to the extreme, because here nothing is sold.

 
NIL Initial Free Distribution

The IFD will be in Q4/2017 and will last 30,000 blocks in the Ethereum Blockchain, which means about one week.

The price for 100 NIL is fixed in 0 ether or, for wallets which don't allow to send 0 ether, 1 wei. Transactions with more than 1 wei will be rejected.

To have more NIL, just send 0 ether again, and so on, until reaching the maximum of 1000 NIL per wallet.

There will be a 40% bonus for participants in the first cluster of 10,000 blocks, and a 20% bonus for participants in the second cluster.

A 20% tip will be reserved to the development team. As long as the NIL price is zero, the tip is literally nil.
